About

The Man Who
Flies History

Paul Beaver is not merely an observer of aviation history — he is a living part of it. A leading historian, broadcaster, and author, Paul has dedicated his life to preserving and communicating the extraordinary stories of the men and machines that defined twentieth-century warfare.

His books go beyond the technical. They humanise history — drawing on decades of personal relationships with veterans, pilots, and eyewitnesses to bring scenes of war, courage, and engineering brilliance to vivid life.

When Paul writes about the Spitfire, he does so as a man who has flown one. When he chronicles the life of Eric "Winkle" Brown, he writes as a man who knew him personally for nearly forty years.

Featured Title

Winkle: The Definitive Biography

"Paul Beaver knew Eric Brown for nearly forty years. This is not merely a biography — it is a testament to a friendship forged in history."

Captain Eric "Winkle" Brown held the world record for the most aircraft types flown by any single pilot — 487 in total. He landed jets on aircraft carriers when it had never been done. He interviewed Hermann Göring. He walked the horrors of Belsen. And he became a national treasure before his death in 2016.

Drawing on Brown's personal papers and decades of friendship, Paul Beaver's biography uncovers new truths about one of the twentieth century's most remarkable men.

Career

A Life at the
Sharp End of History

1970s
The Beginning
Paul begins his research career writing about aircraft carriers and naval aviation. His first book, Ark Royal, is shaped by advice from a certain test pilot named Eric Brown.
1980s
Jane's Defence Weekly
Paul joins Jane's Information Group, rising to Publisher and Editor-in-Chief of Jane's Defence Weekly — the most authoritative defence publication in the world.
1990
Gulf War Broadcasting
The liberation of Kuwait makes Paul a household name as a broadcaster. His authoritative analysis draws a global audience across Sky News, BBC, and CNN International.
1994
D-Day & VE-Day Coverage
Paul co-presents CNN's 50th anniversary coverage of D-Day from Normandy, and VE-Day from Moscow — living history at its most vivid.
1997–99
War Correspondent
Front-line reporting in Bosnia, Kosovo, and Sierra Leone for Sky News. Paul's documentaries bring conflict reporting of the highest standard to British audiences.
2011
First Spitfire Flight
Paul takes to the skies in a historic Spitfire — becoming not just its historian, but its pilot. The Mustang follows. He founds Monty's Messenger Ltd to preserve vintage aircraft.
2015–25
A Decade of Major Works
Spitfire People (2015), Winkle (2019), Spitfire Evolution, and Mitchell: Father of the Spitfire (2025) cement Paul's place as Britain's foremost aviation historian.
